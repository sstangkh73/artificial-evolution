# Research Skill Pack Instructions

This workspace includes a set of research-oriented AI skills.

## Available Skills

- `deep-research`: Use for structured topic research, research planning, evidence gathering, and research memos.
- `scientific-research`: Use for research questions, hypotheses, experimental design, metrics, controls, and validity checks.
- `paper-writing`: Use for academic reports, research papers, abstracts, IMRaD drafts, limitations, and future work.
- `log-reading`: Use for logs, diagnostics, simulation outputs, console traces, metrics tables, and failure analysis.
- `thinking-synthesis`: Use for messy notes, decision-making, idea compression, reasoning summaries, and next-action extraction.
- `alife-research`: Use for artificial life, evolutionary simulations, agent-based models, emergent behavior, energy/reproduction dynamics, and simulated ecology.

## Global Rules

- State which skill is being used when it helps clarity.
- Never fabricate citations, log lines, experimental results, metrics, or papers.
- Separate observation, interpretation, and conclusion.
- Prefer tables for hypotheses, metrics, logs, and experiment designs.
- Prefer short next steps over vague advice.
- When evidence is incomplete, say exactly what is missing.
- For research writing, be formal, precise, and honest.

## Suggested Workflow for Research Projects

1. Use `thinking-synthesis` to clean messy ideas.
2. Use `deep-research` to understand background and related work.
3. Use `scientific-research` to design hypotheses and experiments.
4. Use `log-reading` to analyze runs and diagnostics.
5. Use `alife-research` when the project involves artificial life or agent-based evolution.
6. Use `paper-writing` to produce the final report or paper.
