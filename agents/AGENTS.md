# Research Skill Pack Instructions

This workspace includes project-local AI skills in `agents\.skills`.

Before doing task-specific work in this workspace, read `agents\.skills\engineering\SKILL.md` first, then read the relevant skill below.

## Available Skills

- `engineering`: Baseline workflow for every task, including debugging, review, post-mortem, testing, security, and performance checks.
- `research`: Use for structured topic research, research planning, evidence gathering, and research memos.
- `experiment-design`: Use for experimental design, controls, variables, metrics, and protocols.
- `hypothesis-testing`: Use for research questions, hypotheses, predictions, and validity checks.
- `paper-writing`: Use for academic reports, research papers, abstracts, IMRaD drafts, limitations, and future work.
- `log-analysis`: Use for logs, diagnostics, simulation outputs, console traces, metrics tables, and failure analysis.
- `management-talk`: Use to rewrite engineering content as leadership updates, JIRA comments, Slack posts, standup lines, email, or meeting talking points.
- `statistics-analysis`: Use for statistical analysis, uncertainty, comparisons, effect sizes, and result interpretation.
- `peer-review`: Use for reviews, overclaiming checks, missing controls, metric quality, and paper critique.
- `presentation`: Use for presentation structure, slide narratives, and talk preparation.
- `artificial-life`: Use for artificial life, evolutionary simulations, agent-based models, emergent behavior, energy/reproduction dynamics, and simulated ecology.

## Global Rules

- State which skill is being used when it helps clarity.
- Never fabricate citations, log lines, experimental results, metrics, or papers.
- Separate observation, interpretation, and conclusion.
- Prefer tables for hypotheses, metrics, logs, and experiment designs.
- Prefer short next steps over vague advice.
- When evidence is incomplete, say exactly what is missing.
- For research writing, be formal, precise, and honest.

## Suggested Workflow for Research Projects

1. Use `engineering` first as the baseline workflow.
2. Use `research` to understand background and related work.
3. Use `experiment-design` and `hypothesis-testing` to design hypotheses and experiments.
4. Use `log-analysis` to analyze runs and diagnostics.
5. Use `artificial-life` when the project involves artificial life or agent-based evolution.
6. Use `paper-writing` to produce the final report or paper.
7. Use `management-talk` when the result needs to become a leadership, PM, release, Slack, standup, email, or meeting update.
